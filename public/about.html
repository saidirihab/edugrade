About this platform

Built for students,
designed by a professor

EduGrade AI was designed by Prof. Rihab Saidi to bridge the gap between classroom sessions. Practical work (TPs) is a core part of learning Computer Science — but students often have to wait days for feedback on their submissions.

This platform changes that. Using advanced AI technology, your work is evaluated instantly against the specific criteria of each practical session, calibrated for your year level, and returned with actionable, pedagogically sound feedback.

RS
Prof. Rihab Saidi
Computer Science & Programming — University of Tunis

I created this platform because I believe every student deserves timely, specific, and encouraging feedback — not just a number out of 20. The AI does not replace me: it extends my reach so that no student goes without guidance between our sessions.

How the AI evaluation works

When you submit a TP, the AI reads your file (PDF, Word, PowerPoint, or code), identifies the concepts relevant to your specific session, and evaluates your work across five criteria: understanding, methodology, technical correctness, clarity, and originality. Each criterion is scored and justified with specific observations from your work.

The system is instructed never to invent information, to calibrate expectations to your year level (L1 vs L2), and to respond in the language of your submission — Arabic, French, or English.

Privacy & academic integrity

Your submissions are used only to generate your evaluation and are stored securely. The AI is instructed to flag — but never definitively accuse — submissions that show signs of academic dishonesty, recommending that Prof. Saidi follow up with an oral explanation if needed.

Once all your TPs are completed, Prof. Saidi automatically receives a summary email with your grades — maintaining full academic oversight of the evaluation process.
